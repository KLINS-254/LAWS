THE TICOH LAWS OF WISDOM
I. LAWS OF FOUNDATION
1. Power begins with knowing where you stand.
2. Position matters more than skill.
3. Timing beats action when applied wisely.
4. Silence reveals what noise conceals.
5. Dependence is leverage; create it carefully.
6. Control perception before attempting control reality.
7. Patience forces the impatient to reveal weakness.
8. Appear ordinary to achieve extraordinary outcomes.
9. The first to act often loses; wait and observe.
10. Protect your boundaries; they define your influence.
11. Scarcity elevates authority; abundance dilutes it.
12. People respect results more than intent.
13. Observation is more powerful than confrontation.
14. Avoid unnecessary exposure; power grows in discretion.
15. Consistency breeds credibility; unpredictability destroys it.
16. Never rely on others to define your value.
17. Control the flow of information around you.
18. Always leave the impression of competence.
19. Knowledge without application is wasted.
20. End every engagement with advantage secured.
— Codex of Power
II. LAWS OF STRATEGY
21. Always plan several moves ahead.
22. Anticipate the reactions of others.
23. Adapt your plan to changing circumstances.
24. Never reveal your full hand too early.
25. Identify the weak link and exploit it.
26. Avoid unnecessary battles; choose when to fight.
27. Maintain multiple paths to victory.
28. Know when to advance and when to retreat.
29. Deception can be a shield as well as a weapon.
30. Use misdirection to disguise your intentions.
31. Never underestimate small advantages.
32. Strength is most effective when hidden.
33. Encourage competitors to overreach.
34. Turn obstacles into opportunities.
35. Observe before acting; knowledge is leverage.
36. Align allies with shared interests.
37. Know the terrain of every engagement.
38. Patience often wins what force cannot.
39. Maintain flexibility in all plans.
40. Always leave an exit strategy.
— Codex of Strategy
III. LAWS OF INFLUENCE
41. People respond more to perception than reality.
42. Cultivate charm and charisma as tools of persuasion.
43. Always appear confident, even if uncertain.
44. Listen more than you speak.
45. Appeal to self-interest, not morality.
46. Praise in public, criticize in private.
47. Know when to agree and when to disagree.
48. Subtlety is more powerful than force.
49. Make others feel important.
50. Never underestimate the power of flattery.
51. Influence grows by controlling perception.
52. Lead by example, not by command.
53. Appeal to emotions first, reason second.
54. Make allies feel like they are the decision-makers.
55. Silence can strengthen your influence.
56. Reputation precedes authority.
57. Use scarcity to increase value.
58. Maintain an aura of unpredictability.
59. Avoid appearing desperate for approval.
60. Influence is sustained by patience.
— Codex of Influence
