THE TICOH LAWS OF WISDOM
I. LAWS OF FOUNDATION
1. Power begins with knowing where you stand.
2. Position matters more than skill.
3. Timing beats action when applied wisely.
4. Silence reveals what noise conceals.
5. Dependence is leverage; create it carefully.
6. Control perception before attempting control reality.
7. Patience forces the impatient to reveal weakness.
8. Appear ordinary to achieve extraordinary outcomes.
9. The first to act often loses; wait and observe.
10. Protect your boundaries; they define your influence.
11. Scarcity elevates authority; abundance dilutes it.
12. People respect results more than intent.
13. Observation is more powerful than confrontation.
14. Avoid unnecessary exposure; power grows in discretion.
15. Consistency breeds credibility; unpredictability destroys it.
16. Never rely on others to define your value.
17. Control the flow of information around you.
18. Always leave the impression of competence.
19. Knowledge without application is wasted.
20. End every engagement with advantage secured.
— Codex of Power
II. LAWS OF STRATEGY
21. Never appear desperate; always create options.
22. Let others take the first step when possible.
23. Always read the room before acting.
24. A calm demeanor masks true intent.
25. Alliances must benefit both parties visibly.
26. Control the tempo; timing is a weapon.
27. Remove emotion from critical decisions.
28. Understand your opponent better than they know themselves.
29. Never reveal your full plan.
30. Adapt quickly; rigidity invites failure.
31. Sacrifice small victories for ultimate goals.
32. Let failures teach more than successes.
33. Observe silently; speak sparingly.
34. Make others dependent on your skill.
35. Never fight when not ready.
36. Appear weaker to lure the overconfident.
37. Keep long-term objectives hidden.
38. Disguise ambition with modesty.
39. Refrain from unnecessary confrontation.
40. Let results speak louder than promises.
— Codex of Power
III. LAWS OF INFLUENCE
41. Build rapport before presenting ideas.
42. Show respect to gain loyalty.
43. Flattery must be subtle and honest.
44. Listen more than you speak.
45. People act to avoid pain or gain pleasure.
46. Control small wins to build trust.
47. Avoid making enemies unnecessarily.
48. Use indirect suggestions over commands.
49. Appeal to desire, not reason.
50. Make the other person feel superior.
51. Show confidence; doubt destroys influence.
52. Highlight others’ mistakes indirectly.
53. Avoid bragging; let actions show skill.
54. Keep promises; trust is fragile.
55. Small favors create long-term leverage.
56. Avoid predictability; keep people guessing.
57. Demonstrate controlled patience.
58. Praise in public, correct in private.
59. Frame decisions as choices, not orders.
60. Influence through visible action, not words.
— Codex of Power
